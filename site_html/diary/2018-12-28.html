Pipes and Glue: Side project 1

Build a web app that provides analysis of crypto and finance. This will provide an interesting and useful service to people I may want to work with and allow me to learn how to crete and deploy a web app with automated components. I will do this iteratively.

1. What do I expect to happen? I expect that I will use my spare time more efficiently if I have a structured plan about what I want to build. The project needs to meet several goals at the same time, so that time and effort is spent as efficiently as possible. There are several goals to achieve at once, ranging from short term, pragmatic and risk averse to long term dreams with catapult/unlocking potential.

The short term and pragmatic goals are:

  • Demonstrate my skills to potential employers if I'm forced to find another job in 2019.
  • Hone specialist skills that I am not using in my current role.
  • Enjoy the benefits of creating something, writing, and playing with new tools

The long term and dream goals are:

  • Become a 'full stack' data scientist with no weak points or blind spots
  • Create a side project that becomes a business or revenue generating
  • Make me more desirable to employers, and therefore unlock a higher salary or a job with better benefits (more flexibility, work from home, autonomy/self-direction, more holiday, etc.)

2. Why do I expect it to happen? Based on previous experience writing blog posts and tackling technical challenges incrementally, I am confident that a large and impenetrable problem can be solved in many small steps. Writing about the steps is a fundamental part of gaining understanding as it allows me to reflect and structure my thoughts, crystalising what I know and providing opportunity to see complimentary or second-order benefits.

3. How am I feeling about the situation emotionally and physically? (Tired, anxious, excited, curious, confident, gritty, 'least bad option', hungry, thirstry) Glad to have a plan, confident that the benefits will be worth the effort and that the reasoning based on experience is valid. Apprehensive about finding the neccesary time to focus on a problem. I'm hungry and probaly dehydrated. Feeling a bit sick from having eaten too much chocolate.

I expect a lot of challenges with time and energy management when my son arrives in January and therefore I need a good plan. Also, it is possible that Blockport will not survice through 2019 and I need to aim to ensure a smooth transition into what ever happens after.

6. Problem statement of problem frame: How do I learn the skills I want or need to whilst balancing immediate demands on my time and energy

7. Variables governing the situation: Numerous. Access to information. Access to my plan. How busy I am at work and at home. How much positive momentum the project has. My ability to solve technical problems.

8. Complications or compexity as I see it: Finding good online resources will be key to making good decisions and make progress quickly. Working for small amounts of time but undistractedly and energetically will also be crucial.

9. Alternatives that were seriously considered and why they were rejected: Alternatives were rejected because they didnt combine enough different goals, and the compounding benefits werent as great. I need to tie together different existing skills, gain new complimentary skills and create something that looks useful to others.
